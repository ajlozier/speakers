Reduce hidden biases

Today, your selection committee is prone to subtle and hidden biases. For examples, studies have shown that something as simple as the name of the applicant has strong predictors on the success of that application. A Cornell study found that:

[b]oth men and women were more likely to vote to hire a male job applicant than a female job applicant with an identical record.
A blind application process prevents this bias and helps you make more balanced selections.
